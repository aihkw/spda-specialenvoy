SPDA Mandate & Legal Framework

Established under Presidential Decree, the Southern Philippines Development Administration operates with a comprehensive mandate to initiate and implement development projects across Mindanao and the Sulu archipelago.
1

Declaration of Policy

It is hereby declared the policy of the Government to foster and accelerate the balanced growth of Mindanao and Sulu archipelago within the context of national plans and policies, by the activation of mass participation in the processes of development to be exercised through a unified responsive agency; democratization of opportunity for improvement and maximization of benefits in the utilization and allocation of all the resources that may be available.

2

Creation of the Southern Philippines Development Administration

There is hereby created a body corporate to be known as the Southern Philippines Development Administration, hereinafter referred to as the "Administration" which shall be governed by the provisions of this Decree. Generally, the Administration shall be the agency of the government which shall be responsible for the initiation and/or implementation of development projects in such areas or fields where other government offices have not taken action or have failed as may be agreed upon between the office or agency concerned and the Administration.

Page 4

Purposes and Objectives

3

Core Objectives

The Administration shall have the following purposes and objectives:

A. Development Projects: Promote the development of Mindanao and Sulu archipelago by initiating and/or undertaking by itself or otherwise, development and/or business projects in social and/or economic fields.
B. Community Participation: Generate and encourage mass active participation and cooperation of the members of national cultural communities in its activities which shall comprise manpower development, provision of material, financial or technical assistance; and initiation, development and/or implementation of projects.
C. Community Life Promotion: Initiate and undertake projects for the promotion of community life through organized social or economic activities and institutionalized schemes of assistance and incentives.
D. Investment and Development: Make investments in any field that would enhance the socio-economic development of the region, the uplift of living standards of the people and their socio-political stability.

Agriculture Development

The Southern Philippines is home to some of the nation's most fertile agricultural lands. The SPDA Agriculture Development Project addresses challenges through comprehensive infrastructure development, technology transfer, and market access support, transforming traditional agricultural practices into modern, sustainable enterprises that enhance productivity by 30-40% while improving farmer incomes and regional food security.

Key Focus Areas

  • Modernize agricultural infrastructure including irrigation systems and farm-to-market roads
  • Introduce climate-resilient crops and sustainable farming techniques
  • Strengthen farmer cooperatives through capacity building and market linkages

Power Development

Reliable and affordable electricity is fundamental to economic growth. The SPDA Power Development Project addresses supply shortages and service reliability through strategic investments in generation capacity (targeting 1,500-2,000 MW expansion), transmission infrastructure, and renewable energy development, ensuring energy security while achieving 100% household electrification across the region.

Key Focus Areas

  • Expand power generation capacity through renewable energy plants and efficient facilities
  • Strengthen transmission infrastructure to reduce system losses and improve reliability
  • Electrify remote and island communities through microgrids and off-grid systems

Infrastructure Development

Modern infrastructure connects communities and facilitates trade. The SPDA Infrastructure Development Project addresses critical gaps through strategic investments in transportation networks, maritime facilities, and digital connectivity, targeting 3,000+ kilometers of road construction/upgrades, world-class seaport development, and expanded aviation infrastructure to integrate Southern Philippines into national and international trade corridors.

Key Focus Areas

  • Construct and upgrade national and provincial roads including Pan-Philippine Highway links
  • Develop modern seaport facilities for containerized cargo and larger vessels
  • Expand regional airports to accommodate growing traffic and attract international flights

Education Development

Education is the cornerstone of sustainable development. The SPDA Education Development Project takes a holistic approach by investing in modern school facilities, teacher training programs, curriculum development, and educational technology, creating culturally-sensitive learning environments that nurture critical thinking and 21st century skills while ensuring equal opportunities for marginalized communities.

Key Focus Areas

  • Construct and rehabilitate school buildings to eliminate classroom shortages
  • Enhance teacher quality through comprehensive training and professional development
  • Expand access in remote communities through alternative learning systems

Energy Transition

The Southern Philippines possesses abundant renewable energy resources. The SPDA Energy Development Project champions comprehensive energy transition through development of 2,000-3,000 MW renewable capacity from solar, wind, hydro, geothermal, and biomass sources, implementing energy efficiency programs to reduce consumption by 20-30%, and promoting distributed energy systems to achieve energy independence while creating green jobs.

Key Focus Areas

  • Develop utility-scale renewable energy projects leveraging indigenous resources
  • Implement conservation programs and technology upgrades across all sectors
  • Promote rooftop solar, community microgrids, and hybrid mini-grids

Public Utilities Development

Access to reliable public utilities is fundamental to public health and quality of life. The SPDA Public Utilities Development Project addresses critical gaps through comprehensive investments in water supply infrastructure, wastewater treatment systems, and solid waste management facilities, targeting 95%+ potable water access and achieving zero open dumpsites through modern waste management solutions.

Key Focus Areas

  • Expand water supply systems and treatment plants for universal access
  • Develop comprehensive sewerage and septage management systems
  • Establish sanitary landfills, recycling centers, and waste-to-energy plants

Housing Development

Adequate and affordable housing is a fundamental human right. The SPDA Housing Development Project addresses the regional shelter backlog through construction of 500,000+ affordable housing units, community upgrading programs, and innovative housing finance mechanisms, prioritizing climate-resilient housing in well-planned communities with complete access to basic services, schools, and healthcare facilities.

Key Focus Areas

  • Construct mass housing projects and implement community mortgage programs
  • Upgrade informal settlements through community-driven development
  • Resettle families from disaster-prone zones with livelihood support

Land Development

The SPDA Land Development Project transforms idle lands into productive economic zones, developing 20,000+ hectares of serviced industrial land through establishment of industrial estates, special economic zones, and agro-industrial parks with world-class utilities and ready-to-build sites that significantly reduce barriers to business establishment.

Key Focus Areas
